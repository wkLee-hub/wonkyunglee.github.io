About

I am an Assistant Professor of Economics at Incheon National University. My research focuses on international trade policy, with particular emphasis on antidumping, export controls, and critical minerals. I also work at the intersection of machine learning and econometrics — applying methods such as causal ML, high-dimensional regularization, and nonparametric identification to trade and finance questions.

Working Papers
  • Trade Effects of Export Controls on Critical Minerals: Evidence from U.S. Import Data
    Wonkyung Lee  ·  2024
    Using an event-study design with two-way fixed effects, I estimate the causal impact of China's export restrictions on gallium, germanium, graphite, and antimony on U.S. import volumes and unit values, separating price from quantity margins.
